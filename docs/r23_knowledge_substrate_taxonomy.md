# R23 Knowledge Substrate Taxonomy

This document is an inventory and expansion guide, not an acceptance test.

R23 knowledge work treats the culture KB as a capability substrate: entities,
works, concepts, relations, contrasts, examples, boundaries, and answer-shape
support. It must not become a warehouse of complete answers.

## Scope Categories

The current public culture surface needs at least these categories:

- Music: Mandopop, Taiwan pop, Hong Kong/Cantopop, mainland rock, songwriter-producer lines, vocal style, album/single form.
- Literature: Japanese literature, modern Chinese literature, Western modernism, poetry, literary concepts, motif and style concepts.
- Film and Theater: Taiwan New Cinema, slow cinema, auteur style, film rhythm, staging, conflict and detail.
- Visual/Design/Photography: modern art, Bauhaus/design history, photography theory, image/media concepts.
- Philosophy and Theory: modernity, memory/narrative, authenticity, language, structure, post-structuralism.
- Urban/Food/Science/Technology/Education/Economics/History/Law Boundary/Care Boundary: these remain thin and need later structured primitive expansion.

## Category Expansion Matrix

The KB must expand by reusable category coverage, not by exposed prompts.
Each category needs people, works, concepts, relations, contrasts, examples,
and negative/boundary primitives.

- Music: singer-songwriters, producers, bands, lyricists, vocal styles, album/single forms, regional scenes, representative works.
- Literature: authors, works, periods, motifs, style concepts, genre concepts, translation/cross-cultural concepts, copyright boundaries.
- Film and Theater: directors, films, movements, shot/editing/staging concepts, rhythm concepts, performer/director relations.
- Visual/Design/Photography: artists, designers, schools, works, image concepts, medium concepts, exhibition/source boundaries.
- Philosophy/Theory: thinkers, concepts, schools, relation/contrast cards, uncertainty and source-dependence boundaries.
- Urban/Food/Everyday Culture: cities, streets, public space, cooking techniques, ingredients, taste/texture concepts, local examples.
- Science/Technology: scientists, works/papers/books, concepts, historical theories, current-science caveats, evidence boundaries.
- Education/Economics/History/Law/Care: institutions, concepts, constraints, high-stakes caveats, and non-advice boundaries before any expansive surface rewrite.
